# Safety and responsible disclosure

Pobratym addresses mental health and crisis-support scenarios. A bug can cause emotional harm even when it is not a conventional security vulnerability.

## This prototype is not an emergency service

If you or another person may be in immediate danger, contact local emergency services or a qualified crisis-support provider. Do not wait for a response from project maintainers.

In Ukraine:

- `112` — unified emergency service;
- `103` — emergency medical assistance;
- `0 800 33 20 29` — Ukrainian Veterans Fund crisis support line.

## What to report privately

Please avoid opening a public issue for:

- a crisis phrase that fails to trigger the emergency route;
- behavior that could encourage self-harm or violence;
- exposure or retention of conversation data;
- a way to identify a user from supposedly anonymous data;
- an incorrect or compromised emergency contact;
- prompt injection or model behavior that bypasses safety controls;
- credentials or infrastructure vulnerabilities.

Until a dedicated security mailbox is established, contact the repository owner through their GitHub profile and share only the minimum information needed to arrange a private report.

## What can be reported publicly

Public issues are appropriate for:

- layout and accessibility bugs;
- documentation improvements;
- non-sensitive feature proposals;
- test coverage;
- performance and compatibility problems;
- fictional examples of ordinary conversation behavior.

## Safe report structure

Include:

1. the affected version or commit;
2. the expected safe behavior;
3. the observed behavior;
4. minimal reproduction steps using fictional data;
5. your estimate of urgency.

Do not include real conversations, phone numbers, names, locations, medical records, military units, or operational information.

## Maintainer response targets

These are goals for the open-source prototype, not a service-level agreement:

- critical safety or data exposure: acknowledge within 24 hours;
- high-impact safety regression: acknowledge within 48 hours;
- ordinary bugs and documentation: triage within 7 days.

## Contact verification

Emergency and support contacts must have:

- an official primary source;
- a date when they were last verified;
- a named maintainer responsible for re-checking them;
- a fallback path if a service is unavailable.

No contact should be added solely from an unverified social media post or copied directory.
